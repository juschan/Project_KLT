About K L Tan & Associates

K L Tan & Associates is a boutique law firm specializing on Intellectual Property law. Established since 1988, we have 30 years of proven experience in providing legal services in all areas of intellectual property law, IP protection and management and related transactional matters. Our services include trade mark, patent, design protection and copyright, brand management, commercialisation of IP.


We aim to establish long-term partnerships with clients. By understanding your business needs and goals, we provide bespoke solutions and effective commercial advice to protect your Intellectual Property rights. Our extensive international legal network means that we can assist in protecting your intellectual property locally and globally.


We work with large and small companies, and also solo entrepreneurs and start-up companies and assist our clients in their IP strategies of protection and management. We provide clearance, protection and management of trade marks, patents and designs both in Singapore and globally.

Ms Kee Leng Tan (Designation)

Qualifications

Advocate & Solicitor, Supreme Court of Singapore
Registered Patent Agent of Singapore
Kee Leng practised intellectual property law since 1980 and founded K L Tan & Associates in 1988.

Experience

Kee Leng qualified as a Barrister-at-Law from Lincoln’s Inn, London and is an Advocate and Solicitor of the Supreme Court of Singapore. She has worked in intellectual property since 1980 and founded K L Tan & Associates in 1988.

She is currently the co-Principal Editor of INTA’s International Opposition Guide and was on INTA’s Board of Directors from 2008 to 2010. In 2002, Kee Leng was awarded the Volunteers’ Award by INTA in recognition of individuals who have made significant contributions for INTA.

Kee Leng has been active in the legal profession and in the IP profession. She previously held the positions of President in Asian Patent Attorneys Association (Singapore Group), ASEAN Intellectual Property Association (AIPA) and Licensing Executive Society Singapore. She is currently a Council member of the Asean Intellectual Property Association and a member of the IP Committee of the Law Society of Singapore.

Other Interests

In her spare time, Kee Leng has been an active volunteer with the Young Women’s Christian Association (YWCA) for many years and was the President of YWCA of Singapore for the terms 1995-1997, 2000-2004, 2005-2009 and 2012-2016. She was awarded the Public Service Medal (PBM) in 2005 for her service to the community.
